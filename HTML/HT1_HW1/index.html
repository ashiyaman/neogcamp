Biography

Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi, popularly known as Mahatma Gandhi, is regarded as the Father of Nation. Gandhi was a social reformist and leader of Indian Independence Movement who introduced the idea of non-violent resistance called Satyagraha. He was born on October 2, 1869 in Porbandar, India.

Philosophy

Ahimsa (Nonviolence)

Mahatma Gandhi believed in the power of nonviolence as a means to bring about social and political change. He practiced and preached the principle of 'Ahimsa' which advocates nonviolent resistance even in the face of oppression.

Satyagraha (Truth Force)

Gandhi's concept of "Satyagraha" emphasized the pursuit of truth and justice throuth nonviolent means. It became a cornerstone of his struggle for India's independence.

Legacy

Mahatma Gandhi's legacy is profound and continues to inspire people worldwide. His nonviolent approach to change has influenced civil rights movement, leaders and activists globally. He played a pivotal role in India's fight for independence from British rule and is often referred to as the "Father of the Nation" in India.
