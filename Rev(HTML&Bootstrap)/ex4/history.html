History of Mumbai City

Mumbai has a rich history that dates back to centuries.

Ancient Period

The Koli, an aboriginal tribe of fishermen, were the earliest known inhabitants of present-day Mumbai, though Paleolithic stone implements found at Kandivli, in Greater Mumbai, indicate that the area has been inhabited by humans for hundreds of thousands of years.

Colonial Era

In the history of Mumbai, the islands faced challenges such as the Maratha Invasion of Goa and Bombay, as well as Mughal invasions of Konkan in 1685, toward the end of the 17th century. During the mid-18th century, under the rule of the English East India Company, Mumbai emerged as a crucial port city, engaging in maritime trade with destinations like Mecca and Basra.

Independence

Mumbai played a pivotal role in the Indian independence movement, serving as the center of events like the Rowlatt Satyagraha of 1919 and the Royal Indian Navy Mutiny of 1946.

Post-Independence

After India gained independence in 1947 in the history of Mumbai, the territory of Bombay Presidency retained by India was restructured into Bombay State. The area of Bombay State expanded as several erstwhile princely states joined the Indian union.

Economic Hub

Over the years, Mumbai has evolved into the financial, commercial and entertainment capital of India. The city's skyline is dotted with skyscrapers, and it continues to be a menting pot of diverse cultures and communities.
