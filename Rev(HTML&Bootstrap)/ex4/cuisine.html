Mumbai City Cuisine

India’s financial capital has earned a reputation as a culinary destination too, serving up everything from superlative street food to creative cocktails.

Pav Bhaji

The bhaji is a hearty mix of mashed vegetables like potatoes, peas, and tomatoes, cooked with aromatic spices and butter. The pav is toasted in butter, adding a soft and buttery texture to complement the savoury bhaji. Garnished with fresh coriander, chopped onions, and a dash of lemon, Pav Bhaji is a satisfying and comforting dish enjoyed by everyone, offering a perfect balance of spice and buttery goodness.

Bhel Puri and Sev Puri

Bhel Puri and Sev Puri are iconic street foods in Mumbai, celebrated for their simplicity and amazing flavours. Bhel Puri consists of puffed rice mixed with an assortment of chopped vegetables, tamarind sauce, and a medley of chutneys. The combination creates a delightful blend of sweet, tangy, and spicy tastes, heightened by the crunch of sev. Sev Puri, on the other hand, features flat puris topped with diced potatoes, onions, and a mix of chutneys—tamarind, chilli, and garlic. The dish is then loaded with layers of sev.

Vada Pav

Vada Pav is one of the most iconic Mumbai street food that’s loved by one and all. It has a deep-fried potato dumpling, placed within a soft bun bread or pav. The dish is generously served with sweet and spicy chutneys, garlic powder chutney and fried chillies. The locals, in their daily routine, often pair this flavourful delight with a steaming cup of hot tea.

Misal Pav

This dish is a harmonious mix of curried lentils topped with poha, potato bhaji, chivada, tomatoes and chopped onions. A spicy delight from the streets of Mumbai, it’s typically savoured with pav.

Pani Puri

These bite-sized puris are filled with a spicy and tangy mixture of flavoured water, potatoes, chickpeas, and tamarind chutney. The explosion of flavours in your mouth with each Pani Puri is an experience that captures the essence of Mumbai’s street food scene.
